Japan Trip 2024

A trip to Japan in 2024 visited Tokyo, Kyoto, and Atami and had a great time at all three locations.

Shinjuku Golden Gai

Golden Gai is a small area of 200+ tiny bars crammed into a network of 6 alleyways. You are all but guaranteed an interesting night-out here.

DisneySea

We were on the fence about going to a Disney park in Japan because we've been to Disneyland a ton, but DisneySea turned out to be a gem. The street-food-stands throughout the park blew me away; the food at Disneyland is simply not this good. Combine that with better-maintained rides and well, let's face it... DisneySea beats Disneyland.

TeamLab Museum

The TeamLab "Borderless" Digital Art Museum was great - probably the best digital art museum we've seen anywhere (and we've been to quite a few).

Kyoto

Kyoto is the place that I dream of returning to, maybe every week. It's everything - the food, culture, architecture, streets, people, etc. create a tranquil vibe that just has to be experienced.

Walk through Arashiyama

An unassuming walk through Arashiyama to Otaga Nenbutsugi Temple became one of my lasting impressions of Japan - something about the foliage, decore of the neighborhood houses, and the way they are arranged. We started at the bamboo forest and then walked through the neighborhood streets to the temple. There was a pottery shop along the way where we picked up some coffee mugs and saké sets; had some serious pangs of regret later about not picking out more things there to bring home.

Walk through Nara Deer Park to Cafe Zuccu

Nara Deer Park is a popular spot to see and feed the deer that bow for treats. You buy packs of crackers as you enter the park for feeding the deer over the course of your stroll. There is a gang of slightly-more-aggressive deer that all hang out around the entrance to shake-down tourists to see if they can head-butt them into dropping entire cracker-packs. Once past that initial riffraff, the rest of the deer have taken to the opposite approach of politely bowing for treats, which is adorable. Luckily, we stumbled on Cafe Zuccu outside the park where we had a phenomenal 3-course lunch that made the whole day for me.

Atami

Finding an Airbnb with an amazing view and a private natural onsen was as enjoyable as it sounds. Could have spent a week just enjoying the onsen, but we took a nice walk to town through a park with a waterfall to hit some cafes, stores, and restaurants. One thing to be aware of is that the onsen isn't guaranteed to be running, as it depends on the water flow from the mountain. I saw maybe 1 or 2 reviews complaining that it wasn't working on their stay.
