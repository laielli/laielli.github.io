sushi rice

  • stove-top sushi rice method scaled for 4 cups (dry) Kokuho Rose Calrose medium-grain sushi rice
  • uses a medium heavy pot

ingredients

rice:

  • 4 cups sushi rice (Calrose)
  • water (see ratios below)
  • optional: 1 piece kombu (about 3x3 inches)

sushi vinegar (seasoning):

Use whichever vinegar you have:

Option A: Using unseasoned rice vinegar

  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) unseasoned rice vinegar
  • 6 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp fine salt

Option B: Using seasoned rice vinegar

  • start with 3/4 cup (180 ml) seasoned rice vinegar
  • add 0-2 tbsp sugar and 0-1 tsp salt only if it tastes too sharp/not sweet enough
  • (seasoned vinegar brands vary a lot - taste and adjust)

instructions

step 1: wash and drain (important)

  1. Put rice in a large bowl. Cover with cool water, swish 10-15 seconds, drain.
  2. Repeat 4-6 times until the water is less cloudy (not necessarily perfectly clear).
  3. Drain in a fine mesh strainer 10 minutes.

step 2: measure water

For Calrose on the stovetop, a reliable range is:

  • 4-1/2 cups water for a slightly firmer grain (good if you like distinct kernels)
  • 4-3/4 cups water for a touch softer (often ideal for rolls)

If you've had your rice come out firm in the past, use 4-3/4 cups. If it tends to get soft/mushy, use 4-1/2 cups.

Optional: lay the kombu on top after adding water.

step 3: cook (heavy pot method)

  1. Add drained rice and measured water to the pot. Let it sit 10 minutes (helps even hydration).
  2. Bring to a gentle boil over medium heat with the lid on.
  3. As soon as it reaches a boil, turn heat down to low (the lowest setting that maintains a very gentle simmer).
  4. Cook 15 minutes covered. Do not stir; avoid lifting the lid.
  5. Turn heat off and let it steam 10 minutes covered.

If using kombu, remove it now.

step 4: make sushi vinegar (while rice cooks)

  • Warm vinegar + sugar + salt just until dissolved (microwave 20-40 sec or small pan).
  • Cool to room temp.

step 5: season and cool properly

  1. Transfer hot rice to a wide bowl or rimmed dish (wood hangiri ideal, but anything wide works).
  2. Drizzle all the sushi vinegar evenly over the rice.
  3. Using a rice paddle/spatula, cut and fold: slice down, lift, and turn to coat without mashing.
  4. Fan or gently waft air over the rice as you fold for 2-4 minutes (this gives the classic sheen and texture).
  5. Cover with a damp towel and keep at room temp until using (best within 2-4 hours).

tips

  • Rice too wet: next time reduce water by 1/4 cup; also ensure the lid is tight and you drained 10 minutes.
  • Rice too firm: next time increase water by 1/4 cup and/or extend the final steam to 12-15 minutes.
  • Seasoning too strong: fold longer and let it rest 10 minutes; it mellows as it absorbs.
