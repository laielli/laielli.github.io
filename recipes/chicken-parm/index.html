chicken parmigiana

  • a classic, no-nonsense chicken parmigiana
  • serves 4

ingredients

chicken:

  • 2 large boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp water or milk
  • 1-1/2 cups breadcrumbs (best: half panko + half Italian-style)
  • 1/2 cup finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • neutral oil for frying (canola, avocado, or vegetable)

sauce:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 (28-oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • salt to taste
  • pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • handful fresh basil (or 1/2 tsp dried)

cheese and finish:

  • 8 oz low-moisture mozzarella, sliced or shredded
  • extra grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • fresh basil or parsley (optional)

instructions

step 1: prep the chicken

  1. Slice each chicken breast horizontally to make 4 thin cutlets.
  2. Pound lightly to even thickness (1/4 - 1/2 inch).
  3. Season both sides with salt and pepper.

step 2: bread the cutlets

Set up three bowls:

  • Bowl 1: Flour
  • Bowl 2: Eggs + water/milk, beaten
  • Bowl 3: Breadcrumbs + Parm + garlic powder + oregano

Dredge each cutlet:

  1. Flour - shake off excess
  2. Egg - let drip
  3. Breadcrumbs - press firmly

Set aside on a rack for 5-10 minutes to help the coating adhere.

step 3: fry

  1. Heat ~1/4 inch oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Fry cutlets 2-3 minutes per side until golden and crisp.
  3. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate.

You're not cooking them through yet - just building crust.

step 4: make the sauce

  1. In a saucepan, heat olive oil over medium.
  2. Add garlic; cook just until fragrant (30-45 sec).
  3. Add crushed tomatoes, salt, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Simmer 10-15 minutes until slightly thickened.
  5. Stir in basil; adjust seasoning.

step 5: assemble and bake

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Spoon a thin layer of sauce into a baking dish.
  3. Add chicken cutlets.
  4. Top each with: spoonful of sauce (don't drown it), mozzarella, sprinkle of Parm.

Bake 12-15 minutes, until cheese is melted and bubbling. (Optional: Broil 1-2 minutes for browning.)

serve with

  • Spaghetti or rigatoni
  • Garlicky sauteed broccoli or broccolini
  • Crusty bread
  • Simple arugula salad with lemon

tips

  • Low-moisture mozzarella: melts better and avoids soupiness.
  • Keep sauce light: crispy chicken is the star.
  • Rest 3-5 minutes: before serving so cheese sets slightly.
  • Leftovers: reheat best in a hot oven, not the microwave.
  • Oil temperature: aim for 350-365°F (175-185°C). Below ~340°F the breading absorbs oil and turns greasy. Above ~375°F the coating browns too fast. Use an instant-read thermometer if you have one.
