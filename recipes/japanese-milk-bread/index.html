japanese milk bread

  • an on-going attempt to reproduce the milk bread I had at the KINOMIYA KUWON BAKERY CAFE in Atami, Japan
  • optimized for dual Oster Expressbake Breadmaker, 2-lb. Loaf Capacity machines, because one loaf is never enough
  • yields two 2-lb loaves

ingredients

starter:

  • 1/3 cup bread flour (King Arthur's)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup water

dough:

  • 6 cups bread flour (King Arthur's)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4-1/2 tsp active dry yeast (Fleischmann's)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1-1/3 cup warm milk
  • 10 tbsp room-temp unsalted butter

instructions

step 1: make the starter

  1. Whisk flour, milk, and water in a small pot until smooth.
  2. Bring to simmer, stirring often, until thickened but pourable.
  3. When it's ready, the spoon will leave tracks on the bottom of the pot. Should take less than 10 min.
  4. Set aside to cool.

step 2: load ingredients

  1. Place all wet ingredients in the bread pans including the starter, splitting evenly between the two pans.
  2. Add the dry ingredients starting with the flour.
  3. Pour the dry yeast into a small, centered hole in the flour created with your finger.
  4. Set crust on medium. Program for basic 2-lb loaf cycle.
