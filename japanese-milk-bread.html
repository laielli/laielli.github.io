japanese milk bread

  • an on-going attempt to reproduce the milk bread I had at the KINOMIYA KUWON BAKERY CAFE in Atami, Japan
  • optimized for dual Oster Expressbake Breadmaker, 2-lb. Loaf Capacity machines, because one loaf is never enough
  • yields two 2-lb loaves

ingredients

starter:

  • 1/3 cup bread flour (King Arthur's)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup water

dough:

  • 6 cups bread flour (King Arthur's)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4-1/2 teaspoons active dry yeast (Fleischmann's)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1-1/3 cup warm milk
  • 10 tbsp room-temp unsalted butter

preparation

step 1: make the starter

Whisk flour, milk, and water in small pot until smooth. Bring to simmer, stirring often, until thickened but pourable. When it's ready, the spoon will leave tracks on the bottom of the pot. Set aside to cool.

step 2: load ingredients

Place all wet ingredients in the bread pans first including the starter, splitting evenly betweent the two pans. Next, place the dry ingredients starting the flour and lastly pour the dry yeast directly into a small, centered hole in the flour (created with your finger). Set crust on medium. Program for basic 2-lb loaf cycle.
